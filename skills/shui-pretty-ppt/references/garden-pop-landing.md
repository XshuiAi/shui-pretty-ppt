# Garden Pop Landing / 花园跳色长页

## Use When

Use this style for:
- self-media tutorials and paid course launches
- creator product pages
- tool, template, and workflow product releases
- education, parenting, lifestyle, learning, and community pages
- visually playful service packages that need stronger conversion moments

## Visual Language

- Full-screen slide sections with a bright orange-yellow outer stage.
- White rounded presentation canvas with thick dark outlines and graphic shadows.
- Grass-green bottom strip, pink flowers, sky-blue and mint panels, and playful mobile/tablet mockups.
- Rounded pills, chunky cards, hand-made product-page feeling, and strong button affordances.
- Big friendly display typography paired with clear Chinese body copy.

## Interaction Pattern

- Mouse-follow parallax objects on the cover and closing page.
- Rotating flower marks, floating blobs, and a small moving bee.
- Bottom page dots and bottom-right next arrow for clean navigation.
- Click-to-reveal module: each button press reveals the next content block.
- Clickable cards that update a detail panel.
- Media tabs for video, screenshot, and example slots.
- Keyboard controls for presenter use.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support.

## Content Mapping

When the user provides real material:
- Course outline -> path cards, module cards, click-to-reveal section.
- Product screenshots -> tablet/phone mockups or media slide.
- Short video -> media slide video placeholder.
- Customer proof -> detail-card or launch slide.
- Pricing or service packages -> launch board.
- Long document -> split into cover, positioning, course modules, detail slides, media, journey, conversion, closing.

## Design Rules

- Keep the style joyful but not messy: high saturation is allowed, but every slide still needs a clear focus.
- Do not reuse the reference screenshots directly unless the user owns them.
- Do not add a top-right slide selector if bottom dots are already present.
- Avoid generic blue corporate layouts; this style should feel like a product landing page that turned into slides.
- Keep each slide full-screen and avoid internal scrolling.
