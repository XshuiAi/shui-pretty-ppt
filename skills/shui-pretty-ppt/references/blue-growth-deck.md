# Blue Growth Deck / 蓝色增长稿

## Use When

Use this style for playful but still polished AI product, growth, GEO review, operations recap, and lightweight business presentation pages.

Best for:
- fintech product explainers
- AI tool demos
- content workflow products
- growth and operations recaps
- creator product launches
- lightweight commercial decks

## Visual Language

- Full-screen pages with no outer gallery frame.
- White canvas with sky-blue top and bottom bars.
- Deep navy display type, large geometric titles, and very bold numeric marks.
- Soft but bold blue-growth palette: sky blue, lime green, blush pink, and deep navy.
- Rounded pills, block arrows, floating bubbles, metric cards, chart bars, phone-like CSS media objects, and table rows.
- The mood is professional, friendly, energetic, and a little playful.

## Interaction Language

- Stronger than the formal report templates, but still controlled.
- Use hover-lift cards, floating bubbles, animated progress bars, chart entrance, chip-driven text switching, and pointer-friendly buttons.
- Bottom arrow, selector, keyboard, dots, and natural scrolling handle page movement.
- Avoid blank-page click auto-advance to reduce accidental navigation.
- Respect reduced-motion settings.

## Content Mapping

Suggested page types:
1. Cover: large product/topic word, short promise, presenter/team, floating metric cards.
2. Style positioning: quote or one strong point plus interactive keyword bubbles.
3. Palette/process: four colored blocks or arrow sections.
4. Data page: bar chart, KPI cards, percentage blocks, concise interpretation.
5. Interaction page: three capability cards with progress bars.
6. Scenario page: media mockup plus use-case rows.
7. Component system: reusable module table.
8. Closing: thank-you page with the same candy-tech visual language.

For real documents:
- Convert finance/product jargon into short modules.
- Put data into cards, bars, or progress strips.
- Use lime for actions and key results, blue for structure, pink for gentle explanation.
- If user provides screenshots or product images, place them into media cards instead of decorative stock images.

## Do Not

- Do not copy reference fintech slide text or screenshots directly.
- Do not make the page feel like a banking dashboard.
- Do not use dark fintech cyberpunk gradients; keep it bright and friendly.
- Do not overuse rounded blobs everywhere; preserve the clean slide-grid structure.
