# Vermilion Civic Deck / 红色汇报稿

## Use When

Use this style for formal civic, administrative, government-adjacent, party-building, department-report, and public-service work presentations. The demo positioning can be described as `行政汇报演示`.

Best for:
- government or administrative work reports
- party-building and organizational work summaries
- annual and quarterly department reviews
- public-service project briefings
- policy implementation summaries
- formal meeting or leadership-facing presentations

## Visual Language

- Full-screen pages with no outer gallery frame.
- Warm paper and misty landscape background, abstracted as CSS rather than copied images.
- Restrained vermilion red, deep red, and muted gold accents.
- Red silk/ribbon bottom wave and corner flag-fold motifs.
- Brush-style Chinese chapter titles for ceremony, paired with clean sans-serif body text for readability.
- Strong red numbered blocks, formal cards, timeline modules, photo-text strips, three-measure cards, and result charts.

## Interaction Language

- Default HTML pages should show the full slide content immediately.
- Use the fixed selector, bottom page dots, bottom-right arrow, keyboard, or natural scroll for page movement.
- Keep motion formal and simple: page entrance, bar growth, active page marker, and restrained hover movement.
- Reserve click-by-click builds for a later PPT conversion or presenter-specific variant, not for the default shareable HTML version.
- Avoid playful bounce, sticker collage, neon glow, or overly decorative animation.

## Content Mapping

Suggested page types:
1. Cover: theme slogan, report type, presenter, department, date.
2. Contents: four major work sections.
3. Chapter divider: first major section.
4. Key work: image-text evidence plus implementation timeline.
5. Chapter divider: second major section.
6. Measures: three or four implementation actions.
7. Results: achievement summary and simple data chart.
8. Closing: thanks, next steps, and reporting footer.

For real user documents:
- Put high-level political/administrative framing on chapter and cover pages.
- Put implementation details into timeline or numbered cards.
- Put photos, meeting screenshots, office scenes, or activity images into image-text strips.
- Put data into simple charts rather than busy dashboards.

## Do Not

- Do not reuse reference screenshots or exact patriotic image assets directly.
- Do not overfill pages with red; preserve warm white space.
- Do not make animation flashy.
- Do not make blank-page clicks hide content, reveal content, or jump to the next slide in the default HTML version.
