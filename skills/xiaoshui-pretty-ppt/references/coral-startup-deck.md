# Coral Startup Deck（珊瑚企简）

Use this style for warm, modern company decks, project reports, startup introductions, work summaries, annual reviews, and team-facing proposal pages that need to feel businesslike but not cold.

## Best For

- company introductions and startup pitch-style decks
- work reports, annual summaries, and department updates
- project reviews, implementation plans, and team collaboration recaps
- lightweight business proposals and internal roadshows
- content that needs clear sections, friendly warmth, and visual momentum

## Visual Language

- coral orange-red as the main identity color, used in large chapter pages, highlights, buttons, numbers, and data marks
- pale peach, ivory, and warm white page surfaces
- soft abstract blob shapes inspired by corporate deck covers, drawn with CSS rather than borrowed imagery
- large translucent chapter numbers for section pages
- white or ivory cards with fine borders, restrained shadows, and rounded modules
- clear sans-serif typography with strong English titles and concise Chinese support text
- simple charts, funnels, stat cards, team cards, and timeline modules

## Layout Grammar

- Use a deck-like long page: cover, contents, chapter divider, project overview, team/work module, data review, problem diagnosis, plan, and closing.
- Keep the cover expressive with coral gradients and abstract shapes.
- Use pale paper pages for content-heavy sections.
- Use chapter pages with large coral background blocks and translucent numbers to reset attention.
- Use cards only for individual items, metrics, tasks, or interactive modules.
- Keep content blocks generous; do not compress a full report into dense dashboard panels.

## Interaction Grammar

Interactions should feel polished and presentation-friendly:

- scroll reveal for each slide
- sticky slide selector for jumping to a specific page
- contents cards that jump to sections
- hover lift on cards and navigation modules
- task cards that update a detail panel
- expandable problem/diagnosis cards
- animated data bars when the chart enters the viewport
- arrow controls for moving through the deck
- reduced-motion support

Avoid overly playful stickers, heavy gradients on every page, excessive bouncy motion, or neon/glow effects. The tone should be warm, modern, and credible.

## Content Mapping

When adapting real user content:

- brand/company/project name -> cover title and top metadata
- agenda/outline -> contents cards
- work sections -> coral chapter dividers
- project description -> project brief and metric cards
- team members or responsibilities -> task list and detail panel
- outcomes and KPIs -> stat cards, rings, bars, or funnel
- risks and adjustments -> expandable diagnosis cards
- next steps -> timeline plan cards
- closing statement or call to action -> final dark/coral thank-you page

## Common Page Types

- Coral business cover
- Clickable contents page
- Chapter divider page
- Project overview page
- Team / responsibility page
- Data review dashboard
- Problem diagnosis page
- Implementation plan timeline
- Closing / thank-you page
