# Sapphire Defense Deck / 宝蓝答辩稿

## Use When

Use this style for formal academic, research, thesis-defense, project-review, and serious workplace presentation pages where the user needs a calm but polished blue-white deck.

Best for:
- thesis or graduation defense
- research summary
- academic report
- formal project review
- methodology explanation
- data and conclusion presentation

## Visual Language

- Full-screen slide pages with no outer grey frame.
- Deep sapphire chapter bar across the top of content slides.
- White and pale-blue academic canvas.
- Strong blue section numbers, compact navigation labels, and restrained badge pills.
- Blue linework, rounded content cards, local reusable academic background assets, soft campus-like visual panels, chart modules, timeline modules, and bottom blue wave rules.
- Chinese display titles should use a stronger calligraphy-style stack such as `STXingkai`, `Xingkai SC`, `HanziPen SC`, and `Kaiti SC`, while body text stays clean sans-serif for readability.
- Formal enough for classroom, thesis defense, leadership review, and research-heavy workplace reporting.

## Interaction Language

- Blank click should reveal the next content block inside the current slide only.
- Blank click must not jump to the next slide.
- Wheel/trackpad scrolling, the fixed bottom arrow, top selector, and section navigation are the explicit page-navigation methods.
- Use step reveals for numbered items such as `01 / 02 / 03 / 04`.
- Keep motion precise and restrained: fade-up, blur-clear, bar growth, and section entrance.
- Respect reduced-motion settings.

## Content Mapping

Suggested page types:
1. Cover: research title, presenter, institute/team, date, campus or project visual.
2. Contents: four main research sections.
3. Background: research problem, significance, and scenario evidence.
4. Method: circular method framework or four-part logic.
5. Thinking: three-part reasoning cards.
6. Process: timeline plus image/material grid.
7. Results: data chart, main findings, key evidence.
8. Conclusion: book-like double-page conclusion or recommendations.
9. Closing: questions and next steps.

For long user documents:
- Put executive conclusions on the main page.
- Split long evidence paragraphs into detail slides or expandable blocks.
- Use image grids for multiple screenshots or research photos.
- Convert tables into chart boards when possible.

## Do Not

- Do not use overly cute stickers or playful pastel collage elements.
- Do not overload every page with decorative campus images.
- Do not make blank clicks navigate away from the current slide.
- Do not use copied reference screenshots directly unless the user owns the images.
